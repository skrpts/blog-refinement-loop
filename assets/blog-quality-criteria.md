# Blog Quality Criteria

These are the six criteria the editorial reviewer evaluates against. Use this reference to understand what the verifier is checking and to calibrate your style guidelines.

## 1. Structure

| Aspect | What "met" looks like |
|--------|----------------------|
| Opening | Hooks the reader with a relatable problem, surprising fact, or direct question — not a generic preamble |
| Body | 3-5 sections with descriptive subheadings. Each section makes one clear point. |
| Flow | Sections build on each other logically. Transitions are smooth. |
| Conclusion | Summarises the key takeaway and gives the reader a concrete next step |

## 2. Clarity

| Aspect | What "met" looks like |
|--------|----------------------|
| Purpose | Every paragraph earns its place — no filler, no repetition |
| Language | No jargon without explanation. No ambiguous pronouns or dangling references. |
| Sentences | Active voice. Concise. One idea per sentence. |
| Paragraphs | 3 sentences maximum. White space between ideas. |

## 3. Evidence

| Aspect | What "met" looks like |
|--------|----------------------|
| Claims | Every assertion is backed by an example, data point, analogy, or concrete illustration |
| Specificity | "Teams that replaced daily standups with async updates saw 23% fewer meeting hours" — not "meetings are bad" |
| Balance | Counter-arguments or caveats acknowledged where relevant |

## 4. Tone

| Aspect | What "met" looks like |
|--------|----------------------|
| Audience fit | Language and examples match the target reader's experience level and context |
| Consistency | Same register throughout — doesn't switch between formal and casual |
| Authority | Confident without being preachy. Helpful without being condescending. |

## 5. Completeness

| Aspect | What "met" looks like |
|--------|----------------------|
| Brief coverage | Every angle mentioned in the topic brief is addressed |
| No obvious gaps | A reader shouldn't finish and think "but what about...?" |
| Depth | Each point has enough detail to be useful — not just a list of headings |

## 6. Length

| Aspect | What "met" looks like |
|--------|----------------------|
| Word count | Within the target range (default: 800-1200 words) |
| Density | Not padded to hit the count. Not so terse that it under-serves the topic. |
